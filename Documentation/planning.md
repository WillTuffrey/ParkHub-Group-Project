# Project Proposal

## Business Case

### Problem statement
Finding the right car park can be difficult, and other map and route planning apps can be unreliable. This is mainly due to parking not being a priority for the users or customers of said software, so they don’t implement it. Having a way to filter the conditions for the car park the user is looking for, from price, space and operation hours and other parameters would be a very valuable tool for both regular drivers and people just visiting the city.
In Bristol, especially around the city centre, parking can be quite hard to come by. This is greatly increased during university term time. The University of the West of England and The University of Bristol combined have around 90,000 students with another estimated 12,000 staff between the two, so it’s no surprise that during term time, the availability of parking becomes even more scarce.
Bristol is also ranked 6th in the UK for population density, and while the city has an excellent public transport system, having access to a car is still very useful. For people who have mobility issues, or who live around the outskirts of the city, having access to an easy-to-use piece of software like this will be game changing.

### Options Considered
Apps such as Waze, MiPermit and Google Maps would be other options for users and competition for us. These apps in themselves aren’t perfect. Finding car parks on Waze is often difficult outside of the US especially, as it only considers roads from a vehicle point of view, rather than what happens when you get on food. MiPermit isn’t bad in fairness, however after using the app for a bit, it’s not the greatest when it comes to showing the various locations of car parks on the map in relation to the rest nearby. Google Maps will take into consideration – to a degree – being on foot and by car, but you cannot use these at the same time, it must be one or the other in a trip. None of these apps have any filtering options for car parks, you can only find by location, and even then, it requires searching an area rather than finding options within a radius.


### Expected Risks
There are likely 3 main sorts of risks for this project: technical, legal and cost.

Technical:
*	Back-end to front-end integration breaking, which could affect the user interface
*	Could require encryption to reduce security risks

Legal:
*	A breach in user data and personal records could result in legal battles

Costs:
*	Running the server
*	A/Multiple devs to maintain and potentially update the app
*	Potential costs of APIs and/or licences
